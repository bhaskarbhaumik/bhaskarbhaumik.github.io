TCS Human + AI Autonomy model (Pharmacy) — Experience + Outcomes together

Five levels of autonomy show how to evolve from human-led operations to supervised autonomy with strong clinical and regulatory guardrails.

Level Operating mode Experience capabilities Core business outcome capabilities
L1
Human-led		 Agents and pharmacists work in multiple screens; RPA assists isolated steps. Basic self-service for status; scripted outreach; manual follow-ups; inconsistent context across channels. Manual triage of referrals; manual exception queues; limited orchestration across verification, PA, and fulfillment.
L2
Assisted		 AI copilots assist humans; better knowledge access; semi-automated documentation. Agent assist for answers and wrap-up; improved digital guidance; proactive reminders triggered by simple rules. Auto-summaries for handoffs; guided work queues; initial ML for exception prediction and workload balancing.
L3
Guided autonomy		 Agentic workflows complete end-to-end tasks within guardrails; humans approve exceptions. Resolve-before-contact for top intents (refills, status, address); personalized adherence nudges; abandonment rescue. Touchless routing for standard referrals; automated PA evidence assembly; auto-order actions where allowed; smarter staffing.
L4
Supervised autonomy		 Agents operate continuously; humans supervise high-risk actions and clinical decisions. Proactive care journeys across patient and prescriber; real-time outreach optimization; seamless channel switching. Dynamic orchestration to minimize turnaround time; auto-recovery from disruptions (inventory, shipping, PA delays); higher QPH.
L5
Autonomy at scale		 Closed-loop optimization with strict governance; humans focus on oversight, safety, and continuous improvement. Near-zero abandonment via anticipatory support; high self-service adoption; adherence programs continuously tuned. End-to-end operational optimization for cost-to-fill and throughput; exceptions become rare and explainable; referral conversion maximized.

TCS implementation roadmap (pragmatic)

0–30 days

Baseline KPIs, select top 5 intents, define touchless vs supervised actions, build knowledge grounding, identify quick‑win automations.

31–60 days

Pilot Foundry orchestrator + 5–7 agents; integrate to key systems; implement audit logs and approval thresholds; run in one region/line.

61–90 days

Scale to more intents and channels; add PA acceleration and abandonment rescue; stand up a “driver fix” backlog for continuous improvement.

Governance, safety, and trust

Clinical safety

Human approval for clinical-risk decisions; allowlists for actions; evidence-based explanations.

Privacy & security

Least privilege for tools; PII/PHI masking where appropriate; full audit trails for actions.

Operational assurance

Monitoring of agent success rates, exception rates, and drift; rollback plan for playbook changes.

Principle

Autonomy increases only when evidence shows it improves KPIs without increasing risk. Controls are non‑negotiable.
